What causes Homelessness?

  • Economic Instability: Poverty, lack of affordable housing, unemployment, low wages, and financial crises can contribute to homelessness. Many individuals and families struggle to afford housing due to economic challenges.
  • Housing Market Dynamics: Fluctuations in the housing market, including housing shortages, foreclosures, and gentrification, can displace individuals and families, leading to homelessness.
  • Systemic Factors: Structural issues such as systemic racism, discrimination, and disparities in access to education and healthcare can disproportionately affect marginalized communities, increasing their vulnerability to homelessness.
  • Criminal Justice System: Involvement with the criminal justice system, including incarceration and release without adequate reentry support, can increase the risk of homelessness among individuals exiting prisons or jails.
